Behind the Camera: A Fresher’s Guide to Film and TV Societies at York


Wednesday, 18th August 2010
James Arden
If you’re eagerly surfing the web looking for information to help you start your exciting new life at the University of York, you’ve come to the right place. It happens to have some of the best opportunities to get involved in campus media in the country. You may be excited to start your degree, but if you’re interested in any aspect of filmmaking, television broadcasting or presenting, news reporting, screening films and media in general, it’s time to get excited about campus societies as well!

The following societies are just some of what’s on offer for those looking to get involved in film and television at York.

The Filmmaking Society

The Filmmaking Society is for anyone interested in filmmaking, no matter how much experience you may have. Aspiring directors will be able to use several professional cameras, and those wanting to edit will have access to Final Cut Pro (the industry standard in editing). If your thing is screenwriting, being involved in the society will give your scripts the chance to make it to the screen. Add in cinematography, production management, post-production, sound recording, casting, producing, acting and much more, and you’ll see there really is something for everyone here!

At the end of the year student films are screened in City Screen Picturehouse in York, so if you want to see your work on the big screen this is the place for you!

The society meets every Monday at 6.30pm in L/N047.

“Script it. Shoot it. Show it.”

Visit the Filmmaking Society's facebook group and YouTube channel

YSTV (York Student Television)

For starters, YSTV is England’s oldest student television station, which is pretty impressive! They have a fully kitted out studio and control room, along with some of the most modern media technology. YSTV produce many pre-recorded shows for those looking to get involved in the studio, either behind the camera or in front of it. They also go out into York to cover events wider afield. Furthermore, YSTV gives students the chance to get involved with large-scale live broadcasting, with annual events such as Fusion and Woodstock being filmed from all angles. Previous members have gone on to work for the BBC, Sky, Channel Four and many more.

The YSTV studio is located opposite the James College computer rooms in the Maths Dept.

“Make it. Present it. Watch it. YSTV. Broadcasting to the University of York and the world for over 40 years.”

Visit the YSTV website and their facebook group

The Yorker – Videocasts

This may seem like shameless self-promotion to you freshers, but Videocasting really took off for The Yorker last year, and this year we are looking to build upon that. Unlike other campus newspapers, The Yorker’s online status means we can really take advantage of video-related media, and we provide the perfect opportunity for students looking to get involved in filming news reports and covering campus events, as well as learning post-production and editing. Videocasting also gives students a great amount of freedom – interested in launching videos focusing on fashion, music or theatre? Anything at all would be considered, as we’d like to get as much video content up as possible! Videocasts frequently get the most hits out of anything on The Yorker, so if you’d like to produce videos seen by thousands of students, this is the place for you!

“The Yorker – make it your home.”

Visit The Yorker's You Tube channel, or email us at Videocasts@theyorker.co.uk

YSC (York Student Cinema)

Founded in the 1960s, YSC is the perfect opportunity to get involved in the world of cinema. Whether you are interested in projecting, writing for the termly YSC magazine, selling tickets, advertising films or just having fun, there is something for everyone. YSC has their own projector room, a full Dolby Digital surround sound system and the best part is, if you actively get involved, you can attend all screenings for free!

All films are in P/X/001 (Physics Department) and start at 7:30pm, with doors opening at 6:45pm (unless otherwise specified). Normal price is £3.

“From star-studded blockbuster to underrated obscurity to thought-provoking documentary, YSC delivers cinematic entertainment to University of York staff and students alike.”

Vist YSC's official website and subscribe to their facebook page

World Cinema Society / York University Movie Society

Two societies for the slightly more obscure movie lover, WSC and YUMS are small-scale but are nonetheless a great place to meet friends and expand your cinematic knowledge. Using DVD projectors, these two societies are a cheap and easy way to spend an evening on campus! Visit WCS on facebook Visit YUMS on facebook

Lastly, it’s worth taking notice of one of the newest media developments at York - The Theatre, Film & Television Department. With unparalleled facilities and state-of-the-art equipment, it’s inevitable that links will be made between this budding department and student campus media. Film and TV is set to expand more than ever at York, so take notice, get involved and enjoy university life!
