What the UGM should mean to you


UGM
Friday, 1st February 2008
Ruth Mosalski
Last night Vanbrugh was filled with those interested in policy and those who wanted to see a good old-fashioned fight between the left and right-wings of York Campus.

For those who would just prefer to know what and why they should be voting, here is The Yorker guide to the controversial UGM.

YUSU VOTING SHOULD NOT BE LINKED TO NUS MEMBERSHIP

A fairly simple motion - the constitution used to say that any Ordinary Member who needed to complete a NUS Postal Vote had to quote their NUS number. As you now have to buy a NUS Extra card, this is undemocratic, and the wording has now been changed to say 'University card'. Simple.

WOMEN’S COMMITTEE OPEN TO ALL

Basically, make Women's Committee an open meeting so that all those who wish to attend, can attend, including males. This was one of the more controversial motions discussed at the UGM as it would allow men to attend open meetings. It was met with hostility after some spoke out against those who would not feel comfortable discussing women's issues with men present.

DON’T PRICE STUDENTS OUT OF COLLEGES!

This would mean that the YUSU President could lobby the University to maintain two rent streams: standard or en suite. It also says that she should try secure a variety of let lengths including 33 week, 38 week, 40+ week, 51 week. For the history of the story, see here

Particularly important to Goodrickites is that this motion also includes that it should create "the college they need and want".

CENSURE OF GRACE FLETCHER-HACKWOOD

Proposed by Tom Langley, YUSU Environment and Ethics Officer and seconded by Matthew Pallas, YUSU LGBT this motion proposes that the Union should be able to: "censure an officer as a disciplinary measure." With reference to Grace Fletcher-Hackwood, the motion asks that she be reprimanded, but not no-confidenced.

At the UGM debate, whether this action was punishment enough for the crime was debated. Nadeem Kunwaar, YUSU Ents Officer, said he was concerned: "with the effectiveness of this idea. The last time this was called it was when Nik Engineer [ex AU President] didn't call Rules Committee. However, Matthew Pallas, YUSU LGBT Officer saying, 'It's a serious rebuke and the only other rebuke we can give. It does have some effect."

‘VOTE OF NO CONFIDENCE FOR GRACE FLETCHER-HACKWOOD’

Proposed by Dan Taylor, the student involved in the 'Chav-D' incident, this one states: "That violence from the sabbatical YUSU Academic & Welfare Representative to another student is unacceptable" and "as such she should be removed from her position." This move would be unprecedented in YUSU history. And one which caused much debate at UGM. See here.

A REVISED ETHICAL MERCHANDISE POLICY

This motion calls for the existing Ethical Merchandise Policy to be removed and a better, more comprehensive guide to be created. This should eliminate the problems incurred with several YUSU events including Roses and Viking Raid. The main issues with the policy are that many of the suppliers on the original list could not mass-produce products within a realistic time-scale.

COALITION 2010: KEEP THE CAP

Coalition 2010 is an alliance of education organisations such as; NUS, NUT and AUT, who are committed to keeping the £3,000 cap on top-up fees fur full-time undergraduate students in England. This motion pledges support to the campaign and calls on the President and Policy and Campaigns Officer to update Senate as to it's progress.

SPORT AT YORK

One for all sports players - this states that the quality and range of sports at York aren't up to scratch. It asks for the AU President to lobby the University to improve standards on both Hes East and Hes West.

YORK STUDENTS DESERVE A STUDENT VENUE!

Quite simply this is to lobby YUSU Officers to get a Student Venue on Hes East.

KNOW YOUR RIGHTS! ENCOURAGE STUDENTS TO JOIN TRADE UNIONS

To raise awareness of the student body that they have the resources needed to ensure their rights at work. It actions: "the President and the Academic and Welfare Officer to work with the Trades Union Congress, and the branches of Trades’ Unions in York, to ensure that students have the resources available to ensure their rights at work." Concerns were raised over the wording of the motion, which Sam Bayley admitted had been missed by Rules Committee and those against the motion, including Erik O'Connor who said he felt that we shouldn't be associating with any Trade Union.

MARKETING - PROMOTION OF DISCRIMINATION

This calls for YUSU to not "to advertise any promotions that make use of, or advertise discriminatory material." This falls after much debate over the posters and publicity at College events.

UNIVERSITY OF YORK STUDENTS SHOULD GRADUATE IN THE MINSTER NOT IN CENTRAL HALL

An interesting one. The two sides of the argument are that Central Hall is not the nicest place in York to graduate, and one that "has the essence of a duck's bottom" according to Proposer Dan Coen. The other being that Central Hall is "the very embodiment of University" argued by Rich Croker.
