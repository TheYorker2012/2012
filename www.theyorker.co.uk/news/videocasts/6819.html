March for the Alternative


Embankment
Sunday, 27th March 2011
James Arden
The TUC's March for the Alternative took place in London on Saturday - the biggest rally to take place since the protests against the war in Iraq.

An estimated 250,000-500,000 people descended on the centre of London, filling Embankment with thousands upon thousands of banners, flags and signs before slowly marching to Hyde Park. Speakers included TUC leader Brendan Barber and Labour party leader Ed Miliband.

The atmosphere was almost like that of a music festival or carnival, but behind the mostly peaceful atmosphere, thousands of people were venting their anger and concern at the proposed cuts that will affect millions. The Yorker was there to capture the march as it happened:
