Statement from James JCRC


Wednesday, 18th June 2008
Ruth Mosalski
A statement has been issued by the James College JCRC after last night's decision to remove chair Chet Khatu.

It is with regret that James College JCRC must announce that, following a vote of no confidence, Chet Khatu has been removed from his position as Chair of James College.

This action was taken in accordance with Article 7.1b of the James College Constitution and a vote was taken by a quorate JCRC meeting on the 18th of June and passed by 10 votes to 2 with 2 abstinences.

It should be noted that the decision to propose a vote of no confidence was not taken lightly and we, as a JCRC, would like to thank Chet for the work that he has done.

However, the vote reflects a majority feeling within the Committee, motivated by a desire to see the continued smooth running of the JCRC. We would like to reiterate that this decision was taken purely on professional grounds, and should not be viewed as a personal attack in any way towards Chet Khatu.

The JCRC, in liaison with YUSU, have decided that due to the timing of the vote, a by-election for the position of Chair would not be in the best interests of the College at the present time. A by-election could not be organised before the end of summer term. The Executive Committee, including Vice Chairs, Secretary and Treasurer will take over the responsibilities of the Chair until the new JCRC elections, which are due to take place next term.

The Committee will continue its preparations for the upcoming Freshers' Week, and it should be noted that the JCRC will continue to run as normal despite this decision. The email chair@jamescollege.com will as of today automatically forward to the officers adopting the remit of the Chair.
