York climbs to 11th in League Table


Thursday, 24th April 2008
Ruth Mosalski
York has been ranked 11th in the 2009 League Table by the Good University Guide, a rise of three places.

The university has risen from 14th in the overall League Table published in The Independent today.

The ranking is determined through a combination of factors: student satisfaction, research, entry standards, student/staff ratio, academic services spending, facilities spend, good honours, prospects and completion.

Oxford led the table, beating Cambridge and replacing their rivals in the top spot.

York topped the Yorkshire Universities, with its nearest Yorkshire competitor being Sheffield, which was placed 26th. York's Lancastrian rivals have risen from 19th to 10th.

The University said of the results: "We are pleased by our improvement in the Good University Guide, and we are particularly pleased that many of our departments have achieved top ten positions in the subject league tables including Nursing, Social Policy and Social Work, Psychology, Music, Computer Science, Chemistry, History and Linguistics."

The Top 10:

  1. Oxford (2)

  3. London School of Economics (4)
