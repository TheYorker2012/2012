Vision editor: "If stays same we may not survive"


Campus newspapers Vision and Nouse are in trouble
Wednesday, 9th January 2008
Lucy Taylor
Student newspapers Nouse and York Vision face an uncertain future after printing costs tripled overnight.

At an emergency meeting on Monday local business Newsquest ended a decades-old agreement to print the papers at cost price, after finding itself in financial difficulties.

The company increased charges from £500 per issue to almost £1500 – half the annual grant of each paper.

Vision Editor Iain Withers said: “It’s deeply worrying for us, because at the moment the cash just isn’t there to pay the new prices.

“We’re all working to try and find an alternative but as the situation stands, the paper won’t survive to the end of the year.”

Newsquest, which also publishes local newspaper The Press, has been forced to lay off a number of employees over the Christmas period.

Yesterday they agreed to a new lower printing price of just over £1000 per issue. But this is still well out of the reach of the two campus newspapers, which already raise more than half their annual budget from advertising.

Colin Hindson, Chair of the York University Media Committee (YUM), said: “It came as a bit of a shock but the only thing we can do is to work together to find a sensible solution.

“The papers have been allowed one more issue each at current prices, so we’ve got about two more weeks to sort this out.”

Possible solutions mooted in the meeting included looking for other printing companies further afield in Manchester or Newcastle or reducing the frequency of the publications down to just one per term.

Nouse Editor Raf Sanchez said: “The Press are a business and they’ve just started charging us at market prices, which aren’t unrealistic. Unfortunately it means that the two student papers can’t afford it any more."
